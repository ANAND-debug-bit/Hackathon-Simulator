<h2>How To Use The Website</h2>

<p>
  It's very easy just open the website and you'll be dropped straight into Step 1: Build your team. Add teammates by name and assign each one a role from the dropdown .
<br>

  ![image](https://www.image2url.com/r2/default/images/1782722337816-002b199d-437b-436b-80b3-a36a65dd8299.png)

After locking in your team, the game randomly gives you a problem statement to solve. You can reroll it once if you want a different one :) ur choice .
<br>

![image](https://www.image2url.com/r2/default/images/1782722466900-e3dd0c1d-98b1-480f-9088-08c3a27af1a2.png)

after this you can pick your tech stack frontend first, and if you've added a Backend Dev to your team, they'll pop up with a suggested backend choice based on what you picked ofc .
<br>

![image](https://www.image2url.com/r2/default/images/1782722587726-587527ee-909a-4758-801a-04609bf5607b.png)

then comes your USP (Unique Selling Proposition) pick one yourself, or click (let the team vote) to reveal fake vote counts and auto-select the winner.
<br>

![image](https://www.image2url.com/r2/default/images/1782722714859-5e2b6c57-5c96-4924-b1f4-8b54b03b4bb8.png)

after that, you drag and drop a list of features into priority order — whatever you put first is treated as what you will build first.
<br>

![image](https://www.image2url.com/r2/default/images/1782722863691-af88d7cf-bc70-40b4-ae9f-3d3c6d14a763.png)

then you can write your pitch: a project name and a one-line description of what it does.
<br>

![image](https://www.image2url.com/r2/default/images/1782722953938-62a927ce-1878-40fc-b8f9-f083b7c9cb09.png)

Finally, you hit Submit, and the in-game judge scores your run out of 100 based on every choice you made along the way — tech stack, USP, feature order, pitch quality, and team size — and either approves it or roasts :) it accordingly.
<br>

![image](https://www.image2url.com/r2/default/images/1782723180019-025667d9-aaa1-4683-b604-c5a72cb97f36.png)

</p>

<h3>Why I made this website ?</h3>

<hr>

<p>
  I made this as a fun, fast way to simulate the feeling of a hackathon --> the rapid-fire decisions (team, stack, USP, priorities, pitch) —> without actually needing 24 hours and a working app. It's meant for anyone who's done a hackathon before and wants a nostalgic, slightly self-aware replay of that decision-making chaos, or for someone who's never done one and wants a taste of what the process looks like :) .

</p>

<h2>Some cool features</h2>

<ul>
<li> neon green theme with a dark ui </li>
  <li> 7-step guided flow with a live progress tracker (Team > Problem > Stack > USP > Features > Pitch > Submit) </li>
  <li> a live commit log at the bottom of the screen that prints every choice you make in a terminal-style changelog, so you get a readable trail of your whole run</li>
  <li> real drag-and-drop feature prioritization (not just a static list)</li>
  <li>a scoring judge that reacts to your actual choices, not a random number — different stack/USP/feature/pitch combinations produce different feedback lines</li >
  <li>input validation on the pitch step — typing gibberish like "jkjkkjk" will get caught and blocked before it reaches the judge , so don't try to act oversmart </li>


</ul>

<hr>

<h3> AI use </h3>
<p>I used AI for the sequencing , list of project ideas and also in deciding that how the judge will score based on the choices  </p>
